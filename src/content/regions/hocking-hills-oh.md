Hocking Hills is southeastern Ohio's best-kept secret — a region where ancient sandstone has been sculpted by millions of years of water into deep gorges, recess caves, and towering rock formations draped in hemlock and fern. The landscape feels more like the Pacific Northwest than the Midwest, with moss-covered cliffs and waterfalls tucked into every hollow.

## The Landscape

The region centers on Hocking Hills State Park, where six major hiking areas showcase the geological drama. Old Man's Cave follows a gorge carved by Old Man Creek, passing through tunnels and under overhangs before reaching a waterfall-fed pool. Ash Cave is the largest recess cave in Ohio — a horseshoe-shaped alcove 700 feet wide with a 90-foot waterfall dropping from its lip. Conkle's Hollow is a box canyon with 200-foot sandstone walls rising on either side of a narrow trail, while Cedar Falls offers one of the most photographed cascades in the state.

## Attractions and Activities

Beyond the state park trails, Hocking Hills has developed into a year-round destination. The Hocking Hills Canopy Walk stretches over the forest canopy on a suspended bridge system, offering aerial views of the gorge landscape. John Glenn Astronomy Park, designated as a dark sky site, hosts stargazing events throughout the year. The region's growing food and wine scene includes local wineries, craft breweries in nearby Logan, and farm-to-table restaurants that have followed the tourism boom.

## The Retreat Appeal

A wave of luxury cabin development has transformed Hocking Hills into one of the Midwest's premier retreat destinations. Properties range from architect-designed modern cabins with floor-to-ceiling glass walls to rustic-luxe treehouses perched above the forest floor. Most feature hot tubs, fire pits, and decks oriented toward wooded ravines. The region's proximity to Columbus (under 90 minutes) and reasonable drive times from cities across the eastern Midwest make it accessible for long weekends without requiring a flight.
