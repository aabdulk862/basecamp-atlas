The Finger Lakes region takes its name from eleven long, narrow lakes that stretch like fingers across central New York, carved by glaciers during the last ice age. The deepest of these, Seneca Lake, plunges over 600 feet and creates a microclimate that sustains one of the most productive wine regions in the eastern United States.

Over 200 wineries line the lake shores, specializing in cool-climate Rieslings, Gewürztraminers, and sparkling wines that rival their European counterparts. Between tastings, visitors find farm-to-table restaurants, craft breweries, and cideries tucked into the rolling hillsides.

The landscape is defined by dramatic gorges cut through layers of shale and limestone. Watkins Glen State Park packs nineteen waterfalls into a two-mile trail that winds behind curtains of falling water. Taughannock Falls drops 215 feet — taller than Niagara — into a glacial plunge pool surrounded by towering cliff walls. The gorge trails around Ithaca offer dozens more cascades accessible by short hikes from town.

Beyond the gorges, the region offers quiet lake scenery perfect for kayaking, paddleboarding, and sunset watching from rocky shorelines. Properties here range from lakefront cottages with private docks to hillside retreats overlooking vineyard-striped valleys, all within easy reach of the region's food and wine scene.
