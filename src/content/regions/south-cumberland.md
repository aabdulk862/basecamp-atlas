South Cumberland sits atop the Cumberland Plateau where ancient sandstone has been carved into dramatic gorges, natural bridges, and cascading waterfalls. The area centers around Sewanee, home to the University of the South, whose 13,000-acre Domain is laced with hiking trails open to the public.

The Fiery Gizzard Trail is consistently ranked among the best day hikes in the Southeast, threading along cliff edges and behind waterfalls for 12 miles. Foster Falls offers a 60-foot cascade with a swimming hole at its base, while Savage Gulf provides multi-day backpacking through some of Tennessee's wildest terrain. The plateau's elevation keeps summer temperatures 10-15 degrees cooler than the surrounding valleys.
