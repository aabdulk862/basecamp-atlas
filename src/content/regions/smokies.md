The Great Smoky Mountains are named for the natural fog that hangs in the valleys, created by the dense vegetation releasing moisture into the cool mountain air. This UNESCO World Heritage Site protects over 500,000 acres of temperate rainforest, making it one of the most biodiverse places in North America.

The region surrounding the park offers a mix of rustic mountain culture and modern comfort. Gatlinburg and Pigeon Forge sit at the Tennessee entrance, while the quieter North Carolina side provides access through Cherokee and Bryson City. Properties here range from secluded ridgetop cabins to creekside retreats tucked into hollows that feel miles from civilization.
