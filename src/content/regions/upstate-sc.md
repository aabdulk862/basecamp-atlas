Upstate South Carolina occupies the transition zone where the Blue Ridge Mountains give way to rolling piedmont, creating a landscape of accessible waterfalls, pristine lakes, and forested gorges. The region benefits from a milder climate than its higher-elevation neighbors, making it a year-round destination.

Lake Jocassee, often called the "jewel of the mountains," sits in a protected gorge accessible only by boat, with waterfalls cascading directly into its clear waters. Table Rock's distinctive flat summit is visible for miles and offers one of the most rewarding summit hikes in the state. The Jocassee Gorges area protects over 43,000 acres of wilderness where black bears, wild trout, and old-growth hemlocks persist. Properties in this region tend toward lakefront settings or forested hillsides with views of the Blue Ridge escarpment.
