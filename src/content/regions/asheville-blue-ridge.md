The Asheville and Blue Ridge region sits at the crossroads of the Southern Appalachians, where the Blue Ridge Parkway winds through some of the oldest mountains on Earth. The city of Asheville anchors the area with its eclectic downtown, farm-to-table dining, and a music scene that punches well above its weight.

Beyond the city limits, the landscape opens into a patchwork of national forests, state parks, and private mountain retreats. Elevations range from 2,000 to over 6,000 feet, creating microclimates that support everything from rhododendron tunnels to high-altitude balds with 40-mile views. Fall foliage here peaks later than the surrounding lowlands, extending the color season well into November.
