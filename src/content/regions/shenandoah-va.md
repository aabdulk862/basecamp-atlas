The Shenandoah Valley stretches 200 miles between Virginia's two major mountain ranges, creating a corridor of farmland, orchards, and small towns backed by forested ridges. Shenandoah National Park runs along the crest of the Blue Ridge above, accessible via Skyline Drive and its 75 overlooks.

Old Rag Mountain draws hikers from across the mid-Atlantic for its granite scramble and 360-degree summit views. Below the park, the Shenandoah River winds through limestone country perfect for lazy kayak floats. The valley's lower elevation means milder winters and an extended growing season that supports a thriving wine industry. Properties in this region range from converted barns in the valley floor to mountaintop retreats with views stretching to the Alleghenies.
