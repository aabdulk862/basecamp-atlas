New River Gorge became America's newest national park in 2020, though the New River itself is paradoxically one of the oldest rivers in the world. The river has carved a gorge nearly 1,000 feet deep through the Appalachian Plateau, creating dramatic sandstone cliffs that attract rock climbers from around the globe.

The park offers over 70,000 acres of protected forest, with trails ranging from easy riverside walks to strenuous rim-to-river descents. The New River Gorge Bridge, at 876 feet above the river, is the third-highest bridge in the United States and hosts Bridge Day each October when BASE jumpers leap from its span. Whitewater rafting on the New and Gauley Rivers draws paddlers seeking everything from family-friendly Class III rapids to expert-level Class V drops. The surrounding towns of Fayetteville and Beckley provide a laid-back mountain culture with local restaurants and gear shops.
