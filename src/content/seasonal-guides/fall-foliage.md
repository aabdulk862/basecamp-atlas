## Why Fall Is Peak Retreat Season

The southern Appalachians put on one of the longest foliage shows in the eastern US — color rolls from the highest ridges in late September down through the valleys into early November. These curated stays put you right in the canopy.

### What to Expect

- **Peak color window:** Mid-October through early November at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 feet
- **Golden hour magic:** West-facing properties catch sunset light filtering through turning leaves
- **Cooler nights:** Perfect hot tub weather with temps dropping into the 40s after dark

### Planning Tips

Book at least 6 weeks ahead for peak weekends. Midweek stays offer the same views with more availability and quieter trails. The Blue Ridge Parkway closes sections for weather — check conditions before planning scenic drives.

## Featured Stays for Fall Color

Each of these properties sits at elevation with unobstructed tree canopy views, a private hot tub, and sedan-friendly access so you can focus on the color instead of the road.

### Glamping Collective Ultra Luxe Dome

A geodesic dome at 4,334 feet with panoramic Blue Ridge views through transparent panels. Watch the color change from your heated floors or the outdoor hot tub.

### Canopy Ridge Treehouse

Perched among the hardwoods in the Smokies, this treehouse puts you at eye level with the turning canopy. The wraparound deck is prime leaf-peeping territory.

### Villa Luna Mirror Cabin

The mirrored exterior reflects the surrounding forest — in fall, the cabin itself becomes part of the foliage display. North Georgia color peaks slightly later, extending your window.
