## Waterfalls Within Reach

The southern Appalachians hold hundreds of waterfalls, many accessible via short hikes from trailheads less than an hour from these properties. Summer heat makes the cold mountain water feel perfect, and afternoon thunderstorms keep the falls running strong through August.

### What to Look For

- **Proximity to falls:** Each featured property is within 45 minutes of at least two major waterfall hikes
- **Swimming access:** Many falls in this region have legal swimming holes at their base
- **Morning timing:** Hit popular falls before 9 AM to avoid crowds and afternoon storms
- **Trail difficulty:** Most waterfall hikes here are moderate — under 3 miles round trip with well-maintained paths

### Summer Planning Tips

Weekday visits to popular falls like Triple Falls or Laurel Falls mean fewer crowds. Always check water levels after heavy rain — some swimming holes become dangerous in high water. Bring water shoes for slippery creek crossings.

## Featured Stays Near Waterfalls

### Glamping Collective Ultra Luxe Dome

DuPont State Forest and its Triple Falls are a 45-minute drive. The property's own trails connect to creek swimming spots accessible only to guests.

### Canopy Ridge Treehouse

The Smokies offer dozens of waterfall hikes within an hour. Laurel Falls, Grotto Falls, and Rainbow Falls are all accessible from this base, with the hot tub waiting after a day on the trail.

### Villa Luna Mirror Cabin

North Georgia's Amicalola Falls — the tallest cascading waterfall in the Southeast — is nearby. Several lesser-known swimming holes along the Toccoa River are within a 30-minute drive.
