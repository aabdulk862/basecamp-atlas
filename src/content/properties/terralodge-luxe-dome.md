## The Experience

Terralodge sits on the Cumberland Plateau near Monteagle, Tennessee, where sandstone cliffs drop into deep gorges carved over millennia. The Luxe Dome is their flagship unit — a 1,500-square-foot geodesic structure that feels more like a modernist glass house than a tent. The 12-foot panoramic window wall frames nothing but forest canopy and plateau sky.

## What Makes It Special

- **Architectural scale**: At 16 feet to the center peak, the dome has the volume of a small cathedral
- **Panoramic glass wall**: Triangular double-paned panels create a geometric frame for the forest
- **Heated floors throughout**: Radiant warmth from bathroom to living area — no cold feet at 2 AM
- **Double-sided fireplace**: Visible from both the living area and bedroom zone
- **Matouk linens**: Hotel-grade bedding you'll notice immediately

## The Space

The open floor plan flows from a full kitchen with island seating into a living area centered on the fireplace. The sleeping zone occupies the far curve of the dome with a king bed positioned to face the glass wall. The bathroom features heated floors, a rain shower, and Terralodge-branded robes. A 65-inch TV with Sonos surround is available but feels beside the point.

### Outdoor Living

- **Private hot tub**: On the deck, surrounded by forest
- **Fire pit**: Stone-ringed with Adirondack seating
- **Outdoor shower**: Hot water, open to the canopy
- **Deck**: Wraps the dome with multiple seating areas

## Best For

- Design-forward travelers who want space and light
- Couples or small groups seeking plateau solitude
- Hikers tackling Fiery Gizzard or Savage Gulf
- Year-round stays — heated floors make winter compelling

## Getting There

From Nashville, take I-24 East toward Chattanooga and exit at Monteagle. The property is accessed via paved roads with clear signage. The final stretch is a smooth gravel drive to dedicated parking beside your dome. No steep grades or tight switchbacks.
