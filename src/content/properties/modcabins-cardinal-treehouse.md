## The Experience

The Cardinal Treehouse is one of six nature-inspired cabins operated by Modcabins, a family-owned Asheville business. What sets it apart is the setting: perched above an ancient rhododendron canopy with 1,000+ acres of conservation land as your backyard. You're 15 minutes from downtown Asheville's restaurants and breweries, but the treehouse feels genuinely remote — surrounded by nothing but protected forest and mountain views.

## What Makes It Special

- **1,000+ acres of conservation buffer**: Protected land surrounds the property — no development coming
- **Rhododendron canopy**: The treehouse sits above a dense, ancient understory that blooms spectacularly in June
- **Eco-friendly build**: Sustainable construction with minimal forest impact
- **Asheville proximity**: Downtown dining, breweries, and River Arts District 15 minutes away
- **Family-owned operation**: Personal attention and local knowledge from the Modcabins team

## The Space

The Cardinal Treehouse is a romantic retreat for two with a king bed, full kitchen, and modern bathroom. The design is warm and nature-inspired — wood tones, quality linens, and large windows framing the canopy. The interior is compact but well-designed, with every detail considered. A loft adds visual interest and additional space.

### Outdoor Living

- **Hot tub**: Private, with mountain views above the rhododendron canopy
- **Fire pit**: Solo stove style, on the deck or clearing
- **Deck**: Elevated with views into the conservation land
- **Trail access**: Walk directly onto conservation land trails from the property

## Best For

- Couples wanting a treehouse experience with easy Asheville access
- Nature lovers who appreciate protected landscapes and old-growth understory
- Rhododendron bloom season (late May–June) when the canopy explodes with color
- Year-round stays — the conservation land is beautiful in every season

## Getting There

From downtown Asheville, head east approximately 7 miles. The property is accessed via paved roads. Modcabins provides detailed directions and check-in instructions. Parking is near the treehouse with a short walk to the entrance.
