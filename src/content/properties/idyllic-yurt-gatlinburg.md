## The Experience

Idyllic Yurt sits high on Ski Mountain above Gatlinburg, Tennessee — a modern yurt that blends circular architecture with mountain luxury. The property is just three miles from downtown Gatlinburg's restaurants and shops, yet the elevation and tree cover create a sense of genuine seclusion. From the wraparound deck, the Great Smoky Mountains unfold in layered ridges that shift from green to blue to purple depending on the time of day and the famous mountain haze.

The yurt's circular design creates a warm, enveloping interior that feels fundamentally different from a rectangular cabin. Light enters from every direction, and the wood-burning fireplace at the center anchors the space during cooler months. It sleeps six comfortably across two king bedrooms and a sofa bed, making it ideal for couples or a small group of friends looking for something architecturally distinctive without sacrificing comfort.

## The Space

Two bedrooms each feature king beds and smart TVs, with the primary suite including a jetted soaking tub in the en-suite bathroom. The open living area flows around the central wood-burning fireplace, with a full kitchen equipped for real cooking — not just reheating. Vaulted circular ceilings give the compact footprint an airy, spacious feel that surprises first-time yurt guests.

### Outdoor Living

The deck is where this property truly shines. A private hot tub faces the mountain views, and the gas grill and fire pit extend the living space outdoors. The uncovered deck means unobstructed stargazing on clear nights — Gatlinburg's light pollution is minimal at this elevation. A record player inside and the crackling fire outside set the evening mood.

## Best For

- Couples or small groups seeking a unique architectural stay in the Smokies
- Hikers wanting quick access to Great Smoky Mountains National Park trailheads
- Weekend warriors from Charlotte, Atlanta, or Greenville (all under 3.5 hours)
- Year-round visits — the fireplace and hot tub make winter stays cozy, while summer brings lush canopy and firefly season

## Getting There

From Asheville, take I-40 East to Exit 407 and follow US-441 South into Gatlinburg. Ski Mountain Road climbs steadily but is fully paved with no steep switchbacks. The property is accessed via a paved community road with dedicated parking at the yurt. Standard sedans handle the route comfortably in three seasons; the host recommends all-wheel drive only during active winter storms with ice accumulation.
