## The Experience

Greenbrier Creekside Dome delivers a glamping experience that feels immersive without the discomfort. The geodesic structure sits on a wooded, park-like property along Webb Creek, just off Highway 321 — meaning you get the sounds of rushing water and the privacy of a forest setting without navigating any steep mountain roads. The dome is five minutes from the Greenbrier entrance to Great Smoky Mountains National Park, putting some of the park's best trails within a short drive. At night, the hot tub becomes the centerpiece: soak under the stars while creek sounds replace the noise of daily life. The host, a Superhost with over 160 reviews and a 4.85 rating, has dialed in the details — from the full indoor bathroom to the mini-split climate control that keeps the dome comfortable year-round.

## The Space

Inside, the dome is compact but well-equipped for a couples retreat or small group of up to four. A king-size bed anchors the sleeping area, with a full-size sofa sleeper for additional guests. The kitchenette includes a refrigerator, microwave, hot plate, air fryer, and sink — enough to handle breakfast and simple meals without needing to drive into town. A full indoor bathroom with an oversized walk-in shower means you never have to leave the dome for essentials. WiFi keeps you connected, and a smart TV handles evening entertainment when the fire pit and hot tub aren't calling.

Outside, the creekside fire pit is the social hub — gather around it after a day on the trails while Webb Creek provides the soundtrack. The hot tub sits on a private deck, and an outdoor seating area rounds out the space. The property shares a 1.5-acre wooded setting with one other dome, but each unit has its own dedicated area and feels private.

## Best For

- Couples seeking an affordable Smokies glamping experience without mountain road anxiety
- First-time glampers who want modern comforts (indoor bathroom, HVAC, WiFi) in an unconventional structure
- Hikers using Greenbrier as a base camp for the quieter east side of the national park
- Year-round visits — the mini-split HVAC and hot tub make winter stays viable

## Getting There

From Charlotte or Atlanta, take I-40 West to Exit 443 (Highway 321 toward Gatlinburg). The dome is located directly off Highway 321 near Pittman Center — no mountain switchbacks, no gravel roads, no white-knuckle driving. The host provides a keypad code for self check-in. Gatlinburg's restaurants and shops are 10 miles south, and the Greenbrier entrance to the national park is a five-minute drive north.
