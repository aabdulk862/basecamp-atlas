## The Experience

The Bird Nest Treehouse sits in a dense rhododendron forest on the outskirts of Highlands, North Carolina — one of the Southeast's most charming mountain towns at 4,118 feet elevation. The treehouse offers a complete private spa experience: sauna, hot tub, and outdoor shower all within the rhododendron canopy. You're 5 minutes from Highlands' main street with its galleries, restaurants, and shops, but the forest density makes you feel miles from anything.

## What Makes It Special

- **Full spa trifecta**: Sauna + hot tub + outdoor shower in one private setting
- **Rhododendron immersion**: Dense evergreen canopy provides year-round privacy and a tunnel-like forest feel
- **Highlands proximity**: One of the Southeast's premier mountain towns, 5 minutes away
- **Elevation**: At 4,100+ feet, summers are cool and the air is crisp
- **Waterfall density**: Dry Falls, Bridal Veil Falls, and Cullasaja Gorge all within 10 minutes

## The Space

The treehouse is elevated among the rhododendrons with a king bed and quality finishes. The interior is cozy and well-designed — not sprawling, but intentional. Large windows frame the dense green canopy. The bathroom is modern. The real luxury is outside: the sauna, hot tub, and outdoor shower create a spa circuit you can repeat all day.

### Outdoor Living

- **Hot tub**: Private, nestled in the rhododendron forest
- **Sauna**: Dedicated structure for heat therapy
- **Outdoor shower**: Hot water, surrounded by forest
- **Fire pit**: For evening warmth and atmosphere
- **Forest paths**: Wander the rhododendron tunnels

## Best For

- Spa lovers who want the sauna-hot tub-cold shower ritual in nature
- Couples seeking a romantic Highlands getaway
- Waterfall chasers — the density of falls near Highlands is remarkable
- Spring through fall (rhododendron bloom in June is spectacular)

## Getting There

From Asheville, take US-64 South through Brevard and Cashiers to Highlands. The treehouse is accessed via paved roads in a quiet area just outside town. Parking is near the treehouse with a short walk through the rhododendrons to the entrance.
