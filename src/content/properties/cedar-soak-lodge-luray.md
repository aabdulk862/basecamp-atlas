## The Experience

Cedar Soak Lodge sits on a wooded acre in the Shenandoah Valley just outside Luray, Virginia — a small mountain town known for its caverns, craft breweries, and proximity to Shenandoah National Park. The cabin blends rustic charm with modern comfort: think shiplap walls, a well-equipped kitchen, and smart TVs alongside a private cedar barrel sauna and year-round hot tub beneath the tree canopy. The property's standout feature is its location — a two-minute walk puts you at Shenandoah River Outfitters, where tubing, kayaking, and fishing launch directly from the riverbank. After a day on the water or hiking the park's ridgeline trails, the sauna-to-hot-tub ritual becomes the evening's main event.

## What Makes It Special

- **Cedar barrel sauna**: Authentic wood-fired sauna reaching 220°F — detox after a day on the trails
- **Year-round hot tub**: Under the stars on the back deck, surrounded by forest
- **Walk to the river**: Shenandoah River Outfitters is a two-minute stroll for tubing, kayaking, and fishing
- **Shenandoah National Park**: Thornton Gap entrance is 15 minutes away with access to Skyline Drive and dozens of trails
- **Local craft scene**: Hawksbill Brewing, wineries, and downtown Luray restaurants within 10–15 minutes

## The Space

The cabin sleeps six across two bedrooms — a king primary and a second room with bunks — plus a full bathroom with modern fixtures. The open-plan living area has a comfortable couch, smart TV, and a kitchen stocked with a Ninja coffee maker, full cookware, and dining for six. The design is rustic-modern: clean lines, warm wood tones, and intentional touches that make it feel curated rather than generic. Life-size Connect Four and cornhole on the lawn add playful energy for groups.

### Outdoor Living

- **Cedar barrel sauna**: Private, wood-heated, seats four comfortably
- **Hot tub**: Year-round, on the back deck beneath the canopy
- **Stone fire pit**: Under string lights for evening gatherings
- **Hammock**: Strung between trees for afternoon reading
- **Weber charcoal grill**: For cookouts on warm evenings
- **Rocking chair porch**: Front deck with morning coffee views

## Best For

- Couples or small groups wanting a sauna-and-soak wellness retreat
- Hikers using the cabin as a Shenandoah National Park basecamp
- River lovers who want tubing and kayaking at their doorstep
- Those seeking a balance of outdoor adventure and evening relaxation

## Getting There

From Richmond, take I-64 West to I-81 South, then US-211 East toward Luray. The cabin is accessed via well-maintained paved roads in a quiet wooded area just outside town. Parking is beside the cabin on a level gravel pad. No steep grades or tight switchbacks — standard sedans handle the route easily year-round.
