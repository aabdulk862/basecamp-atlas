## The Experience

The Chalets at Old Man's Cave occupy 140 acres of Hocking Hills forest along State Route 664, placing you at the geographic heart of Ohio's most dramatic hiking landscape. Hemlock is a log cabin chalet that sits in a grassy clearing surrounded by woods — private enough to feel secluded, yet just two miles from the Hocking Hills State Park visitors center and the trailhead for Old Man's Cave. The cabin's standout feature is its indoor sauna, a rarity in the region, paired with a private outdoor hot tub steps from the covered porch. After a day exploring sandstone gorges and waterfalls, the sauna-to-hot-tub ritual is the kind of recovery that makes you want to extend your stay.

## What Makes It Special

- **Indoor sauna**: A private sauna inside the cabin — uncommon in Hocking Hills rentals
- **Hot tub on the porch**: Step from the covered porch directly into the hot tub, ideal in winter
- **Two miles from Old Man's Cave**: The closest you can stay to the park's most iconic trail
- **140-acre wooded campus**: Part of the Chalets resort property with forest trails and seasonal pool
- **Log cabin character**: Vaulted ceilings with skylights, wood floors, and a gas fireplace

## The Space

Hemlock is a two-bedroom, two-bathroom log cabin with 1,700 square feet spread across two levels. The main floor features a queen bedroom, full kitchen with stainless appliances, and a living area with vaulted ceilings and skylights that flood the space with natural light. The lower level has a second bedroom with two queen beds and its own full bathroom. The gas fireplace operates seasonally and adds warmth on cool evenings. The kitchen is fully equipped for real cooking — not just reheating — with a full-size range, refrigerator, microwave, and all the utensils you need.

## Best For

- Hikers who want to be steps from the best trails in the Midwest
- Couples or small groups seeking the sauna-and-hot-tub recovery ritual
- Four-season visitors — the cabin works year-round with heat, A/C, and indoor amenities
- Those who want proximity to restaurants and a general store without sacrificing a wooded setting

## Getting There

From Columbus, take US-33 South to Logan, then State Route 664 South. The Chalets campus is directly off SR 664 — a fully paved state highway with no steep grades or unpaved sections. The driveway is flat and paved with parking beside your cabin. The general store is next door, and the state park visitors center is a five-minute drive south on the same road.
