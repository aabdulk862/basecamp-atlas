## The Experience

Green Creek Shipyard 2.0 proves that a 40-foot shipping container can be transformed into something genuinely luxurious. Built by three siblings on family land in the Blue Ridge foothills of North Carolina, the container has been reimagined with a custom waterfall feature, sunk-in hammock, swinging daybed, and a level of finish that belies its industrial origins. It's the closest property to Charlotte on the escape vertical — just 90 minutes door to door.

## What Makes It Special

- **Industrial-meets-nature**: Steel container exterior contrasts with the lush mountain setting
- **Custom waterfall feature**: Built into the property's landscaping
- **Sunk-in hammock**: Recessed into the deck for a cocoon-like lounging experience
- **Swinging daybed**: Suspended on the deck for afternoon naps
- **Proximity**: 90 minutes from Charlotte, 30 from Asheville, 4 miles from the Blue Ridge Parkway

## The Space

The container interior is compact but thoughtfully designed. A king bed faces a large glass window — imagine watching sunrise without leaving bed. The glass rain shower is a standout detail in the small bathroom. Storage and surfaces are minimal but sufficient for a weekend stay. The real living space is outside on the expansive deck.

## Best For

- Design enthusiasts who appreciate adaptive reuse and creative architecture

## Getting There

From Charlotte, take I-26 West toward Columbus, NC. The property is in the Green Creek community of Polk County — all paved roads with a short gravel drive to dedicated parking. The Blue Ridge Parkway is 4 miles away. Asheville is 25 minutes further west.
