## The Experience

Villa Luna's Mirror Cabin is an architectural statement piece hidden in the North Georgia mountains near Dahlonega. The exterior is clad in one-way mirrored glass panels that reflect the surrounding forest — from the outside, the cabin effectively disappears into the tree line. From the inside, every wall is a floor-to-ceiling window into the woods. The effect is surreal: you're simultaneously invisible and completely exposed to nature.

## What Makes It Special

- **Disappearing architecture**: One-way mirror cladding makes the cabin invisible from outside
- **Copper soaking tub**: Freestanding on the deck, overlooking a private creek below
- **Outdoor shower**: Hot water, stone floor, open to the sky and forest
- **Sound design**: The creek provides constant ambient sound — no white noise machine needed
- **Curated details**: Vinyl record collection, local wine in the mini bar, handmade ceramics

## The Space

The cabin is a single 450-square-foot room with intentional zones. The sleeping area centers on a king bed facing the largest glass wall. A sitting nook with a leather daybed and record player occupies one corner. The bathroom is semi-open with a rainfall shower, heated stone floors, and a vanity with locally sourced toiletries.

### Outdoor Living

- **Deck**: Wraps three sides of the cabin with the copper soaking tub as centerpiece
- **Fire pit**: Stone-ringed, set back from the cabin near the creek bank
- **Creek access**: A short stone staircase leads to a shallow swimming hole
- **Outdoor shower**: Hot water, river stone floor, surrounded by mountain laurel

## Best For

- Design-conscious travelers who appreciate architectural experiences
- Couples celebrating anniversaries or special occasions
- Photographers and creatives seeking visual inspiration
- Year-round stays — the mirror effect is stunning in every season

## Getting There

From Atlanta, the drive is about 90 minutes north on GA-400 and US-19. The property is accessed via a paved two-lane road that winds through the foothills. The final quarter-mile is a smooth gravel drive to a dedicated parking area. The cabin is a 2-minute flat walk from the car — no stairs or steep terrain.
